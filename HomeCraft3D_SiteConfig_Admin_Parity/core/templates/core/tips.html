Tips & Tricks

A practical quick-start for better 3D prints. (Static for now — this will become the Blog later.)
First-layer success
  • Clean your build plate and re-level when prints stop sticking.
  • Slow the first layer and use a slightly higher nozzle temp for PLA/PETG.
  • Check Z-offset: too high = poor adhesion; too low = ridges and elephant foot.
Warping & cooling
  • Use a brim for tall/thin parts.
  • Avoid drafts; consider an enclosure for ABS/ASA.
  • Dial part cooling: too much can cause layer separation on some materials.
Wet filament symptoms
  • Popping/crackling, stringing, and rough surfaces can mean moisture.
  • Store spools sealed with desiccant.
  • Dry filament when needed (PLA/PETG especially in humid climates).
Supports & orientation
  • Rotate parts to minimize supports on visible faces.
  • Use tree supports for organic shapes when your slicer supports it.
  • Tune support interface density for easier removal and better undersides.
Pro tip
When troubleshooting, change one variable at a time (speed, temp, retraction, cooling, etc.). Keep a small note of what you changed and the outcome.
